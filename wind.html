Wind Satellite

The Wind satellite, launched in 1994, is a critical component of NASA's mission to study the solar wind and its interaction with Earth's magnetosphere. Positioned at the L1 Lagrangian point, Wind continuously monitors the solar wind's properties, including its speed, density, and temperature. This data is vital for understanding space weather phenomena that can impact satellite operations, communications, and power systems on Earth. Wind is part of the International Solar Terrestrial Physics (ISTP) program, which aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the Sun-Earth connection and the dynamics of the solar system.
